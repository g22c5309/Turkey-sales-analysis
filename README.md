# E-Commerce Customer Behavior & Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This project performs an **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on an e-commerce customer behaviour and sales dataset. The goal is to understand purchasing patterns, customer engagement, and regional performance in order to generate actionable business insights.

The analysis focuses on identifying key revenue drivers, evaluating customer satisfaction, and examining how factors such as product category, device usage, and geographic location influence sales performance.

---

## Objectives

The main questions explored in this analysis include:

* Who are the most valuable customers?
* How do product categories influence revenue and order volume?
* Which cities contribute the most to sales performance?
* What role do device types play in customer purchasing behaviour?
* How do repeat customers contribute to overall revenue?
* Are customer satisfaction levels consistent across regions?

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis

The analysis includes several visualisations designed to understand customer behaviour and sales performance.

### 1. Revenue Over Time

Analyses how revenue changes over time to identify sales trends and possible seasonal patterns.

### 2. Revenue by Product Category

Examines which product categories generate the highest revenue.

### 3. Number of Orders by Category

Compares customer demand across different product categories.

### 4. Device Type Performance

Compares revenue and order volume across different device types to understand purchasing channels.

### 5. City-Level Sales Performance

* **Orders by City (Bar Chart)**
* **Revenue Share by City (Pie Chart)**

This analysis highlights geographic market concentration. Istanbul emerged as the strongest market in both revenue and order volume.

### 6. Customer Satisfaction by City

Average ratings were compared across cities to evaluate whether customer satisfaction differs geographically.

### 7. Repeat vs One-Time Customers

Examines revenue contribution from returning customers versus first-time buyers to assess retention performance.

---

## Key Insights

* **Mobile devices generated the highest number of orders and total revenue**, indicating that mobile commerce is the dominant purchasing channel.
* **Istanbul is the top-performing city**, contributing the highest revenue and order volume.
* Revenue is geographically concentrated in major urban areas.
* Customer satisfaction remains **consistent across cities**, suggesting stable service quality.
* **Repeat customers contribute significantly to total revenue**, highlighting the importance of customer retention strategies.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, several strategic recommendations can be made:

* **Prioritise mobile optimisation** to improve user experience and increase conversions.
* **Strengthen customer retention programs** such as loyalty incentives and personalised marketing.
* **Expand marketing efforts in lower-performing cities** to reduce geographic revenue concentration.
* **Leverage high-performing product categories** through targeted promotions and inventory optimisation.
* **Continue monitoring customer satisfaction**, particularly in high-volume markets.
